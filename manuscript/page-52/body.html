In fact, of all the planets in the Solar System, the seasons of Mars are the most Earth-like. Only the length of a Martian season is twice of that on Earth. Surface temperature of Mars ranges from about −143 °C (−225 °F) during winters at the poles to ~35 °C (95 °F) during summer at the equator. On an average, Mars is much colder than our Antartican South Pole.

The Martian surface is very feature-rich and remarkable. It has the largest known volcano of our Solar System – The Olympus Mons - that is about 375 miles wide and ~69,000 feet high. It has a grander canyon - The Valles Marineris - that is ~2,500 miles long and up to 4 miles deep. In comparison the Grand Canyon of Arizona is only ~500 miles in length and only a mile deep.
