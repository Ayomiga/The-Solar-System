Eclipse

Now that we have covered the Sun, the Moon and the Earth let's take a look at Eclipses:

An eclipse is an astronomical event that occurs when a heavenly object is temporarily obscured, either by passing into the shadow of another body or by having another body pass between it and the observer.

On Earth we get to see two kinds of eclipses:

  1. A Solar eclipse — in which the sun is obscured by the moon.
  2. Lunar eclipse — in which the moon appears darkened as it passes into the earth's shadow.

There are three types solar and lunar eclipses too — total, partial and penumbral — the most dramatic being a total lunar eclipse, in which Earth's shadow completely covers the moon. Eclipses and other interesting configurations or alignments of objects are also loosely called a Syzygy.
